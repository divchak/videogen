> 📜 **VERSE MEANING (Full 11 Verses — for reference):**
> This is the complete Thiruthondar Thogai by Sundarar. Each verse lists groups among the 63 Nayanmars and ends with "ஆரூரன் ஆரூரில் அம்மானுக் காளே" (I am the servant/slave of the Lord of Aarur / Thiruvarur). 
> Verse 1 begins by honoring the Thillai Anthanars (Dikshitars) of Chidambaram and other early saints. 
> The hymn continues through iconic Nayanmars such as Kannappar (hunter who offered his eye), Appar (Thirunavukkarasar), Sambandar, and many others. 
> Verse 11 closes with Sundarar referring to himself and declaring that those who love and serve the Lord of Aarur (and by extension all the Nayanmars and Nataraja) will be blessed. 
> The entire work is a profound expression of humility ("adiyen") and the sacred community of Shiva's greatest devotees. It is the foundational text that later inspired Sekkizhar's Periyapuranam. 
> — Sundarar, 7th Tirumurai, Thiruthondar Thogai (complete).
