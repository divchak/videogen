━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━
"Thodudaya Seviyan" (தோடுடைய செவியன்) means "He whose ears are adorned
with sacred ornaments" — one of the many poetic epithets of Lord Shiva.

Composed by Thirugnana Sambandar (7th century CE) — the child saint who
received divine milk from Goddess Parvati herself as an infant, and from
that moment composed perfect Thevaram hymns that are now part of the
holiest Shaiva canon in Tamil literature: the 12 Tirumurai.

This hymn describes Lord Shiva at Thiruvarur — the great Thyagaraja temple
that is one of the Paadal Petra Sthalams (divine abodes celebrated in
Thevaram). The lyrics paint Shiva's sacred paradoxical nature:
🔱 The one who destroyed the three demon cities (Tripura Dahanam)
🌊 The one with Ganga flowing in his sacred matted hair (Gangadhara)
🌙 The one who wears the crescent moon as ornament
💙 The one who swallowed cosmic poison to save all existence (Neelakanta)

🔱 ABOUT THIRUGNANA SAMBANDAR
━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━
Thirugnana Sambandar (திருஞான சம்பந்தர்) was born in Sirkazhi, Tamil Nadu,
in the 7th century CE. He is one of the most celebrated of the 63 Nayanmars
— the Tamil Shaiva saint-devotees — and his compositions form the first
three books (1st, 2nd, 3rd Tirumurai) of the Shaiva Tamil canon.

As an infant, he was left alone at the temple tank by his father. He began
to cry from hunger. Goddess Parvati herself descended and gave him divine
milk — from that moment he received divine wisdom and began composing
perfect Thevaram hymns at the age of three.

He sang over 4,000 hymns across 282 Tamil Shaiva temples. He is considered
the most emotionally intense and musically gifted of all Thevaram composers.

"Thodudaya Seviyan" is one of his most celebrated hymns — sung for 1,300
years in every major Tamil Shaiva temple, including the great Thiruvarur
Thyagaraja Temple where this video is set.

━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━
🏛️ ABOUT THIRUVARUR THYAGARAJA TEMPLE
━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━
The Thiruvarur Thyagaraja Temple is one of the oldest and greatest Shiva
temples in Tamil Nadu — dedicated to Lord Thyagaraja (a form of Shiva) and
Goddess Kamalambal. It is:
• One of the Paadal Petra Sthalams — temples celebrated in Thevaram
• One of the Pancha Sabhas — the five cosmic dance stages of Nataraja
• The Veethi Vimana Sthalam — where the divine chariot procession is supreme
• The largest temple chariot festival in Tamil Nadu is held here annually

━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━
🎵 ABOUT THE NATTAI RAGA
━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━
This Thevaram is traditionally sung in Nattai raga — one of the oldest and
most sacred ragas in Carnatic music. Nattai raga is considered the raga of
Lord Shiva himself: fierce, majestic, and capable of conveying both the
terrifying power of Shiva the destroyer and the infinite grace of Shiva
the protector — the perfect sonic match for this sacred paradoxical hymn.
