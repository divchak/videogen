## 4.5 Vocal Direction

```
CHILD LEAD VOICE (Velu — warm, slightly breathless from running):
- "Ammammaa!" — must sound like an actual child calling their grandmother
- Counting section: warm and personal — like listing treasures, not numbers
- Bridge: softer, almost sleepy — the voice of a child nearly asleep in Paatti's arms
- "Meendum vaa Paatti!" — pleading but trusting — the most touching vocal moment

PAATTI VOICE (Alto, warm, old, beloved):
- The warmest voice ever recorded for Tamil kids content
- "Naan meendum varuven" — absolute certainty, absolute love
- Should sound exactly like everyone's actual grandmother

CHILDREN'S CHORUS:
- Gentle, warm echo — never loud — this song requires restraint
- "Ammammaa!" x4 building: start whisper, end with full warmth (not shout)

SOUND DESIGN (CRITICAL):
- Village ambient: must include: rooster, wind through mango leaves, distant temple bell
- Bus sound transitioning to village silence = the journey told in 3 seconds of audio
- The mango leaves in the bridge section: very light rustling over the quiet emotional strings
- "Ammammaa!" should have a very slight room reverb — like being called in a Tamil home courtyard
```
