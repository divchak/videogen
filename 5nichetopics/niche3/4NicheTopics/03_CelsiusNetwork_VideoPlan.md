## a) 🎬 YOUTUBE SCRIPT (Full Narration ~1,050 words)

**[HOOK — 0:00–0:30]**
"June 12, 2022. Millions of people wake up, open their phones, and discover something terrifying: their crypto savings account is locked. No withdrawal button. Just a message — 'Celsius is pausing all withdrawals, swaps, and transfers due to extreme market conditions.' No timeline. No amount. Just: your money is in here, and you can't have it. Over 1.7 million customers. And the man who built this company? He'd been on YouTube just weeks earlier, telling everyone it was totally fine."

**[BACKGROUND — 0:30–1:30]**
"Celsius Network was founded in 2017 by Alex Mashinsky, who had a genuine track record — he'd co-invented Voice over IP technology in the 1990s. His pitch for Celsius was seductive and simple: the big banks earn 20–30% on your deposits. They hand you back 0.5%. Celsius would cut out the middlemen and pass those returns straight to you. Up to 18% annual yield. On your crypto. While you sleep.

It sounded revolutionary. It was marketed as a revolution. 'Be Your Own Bank' was the tagline. Mashinsky did a weekly YouTube show — in a Celsius t-shirt, sitting behind a Celsius mug — answering questions directly from customers. By 2022, Celsius had $20 billion in assets under management and 1.7 million users worldwide. Mashinsky appeared on CNBC and at conferences. He called himself 'the CEO who puts his money where his mouth is.' We'll come back to that phrase."

**[HOW IT ACTUALLY WORKED — 1:30–2:30]**
"So how did Celsius actually generate those returns? It deployed your deposits into high-risk decentralized finance protocols, lending markets, and volatile crypto yield strategies. It made enormous concentrated bets on assets like stETH — a derivative of Ethereum that couldn't easily be converted back. When things were good, the returns held up. When things weren't good, there was no plan.

Here's what Celsius was not: a bank. There was no FDIC insurance. No reserve requirements mandated by a regulator. No independent auditor verifying the books. No requirement to tell you what specifically your money was being used for. Celsius was structured like a hedge fund, operated like a casino, and marketed like a savings account. That combination works right up until the moment it doesn't."

**[THE COLLAPSE — 2:30–3:45]**
"When crypto markets crashed in May 2022, the cracks became craters. Celsius's stETH holdings couldn't be quickly sold. Losses were mounting from bad loans and failed DeFi strategies. Liquidity was draining. And on June 12 — without warning, without a phone call to customers, without even a tweet ahead of time — Celsius posted a blog update saying all withdrawals were frozen indefinitely.

For 1.7 million people, this was catastrophic. Many had their entire savings locked inside. Some had treated Celsius the way their parents treated a savings account — reliable, boring, safe. In July 2022, Celsius filed for Chapter 11 bankruptcy. Court documents that spilled out afterward revealed what the company had known internally for weeks: a $1.2 billion hole in its balance sheet. And most damningly — just weeks before the freeze, senior executives had quietly withdrawn millions of their own funds from the platform. While telling customers everything was fine."

**[MASHINSKY — FRAUD EXPOSED — 3:45–4:45]**
"Alex Mashinsky was not just a reckless risk-taker. According to federal prosecutors, he was a deliberate fraudster. In 2023, Mashinsky was arrested and charged with securities fraud, commodities fraud, and market manipulation. The indictment alleged that he had secretly sold $68 million of his own CEL tokens — the platform's native coin — while publicly telling customers that Celsius was healthy and that CEL was undervalued and they should buy more. That is the definition of 'the CEO who puts his money where his mouth is' — as long as his mouth is saying one thing while his hands are doing another."

**[CUSTOMER RECOVERY — 4:45–5:30]**
"The bankruptcy proceedings were long and painful. Creditors fought over scraps for over a year. In 2024, a recovery plan was approved — customers would receive approximately 67 to 72 cents for every dollar they had deposited. At first glance that sounds almost reasonable. But here's the catch: many customers had deposited at peak crypto prices. Getting 72 cents back at today's prices, on an asset that was worth more when you put it in, means the real-world loss is far higher than the percentage suggests. For some customers — those who used Celsius as their primary financial cushion — even partial recovery came too late."

**[LESSONS — 5:30–6:15]**
"Celsius is a masterclass in weaponized banking language. The words 'deposit,' 'yield,' 'savings,' 'earn' — all borrowed from an industry built on regulation, insurance, and reserve requirements. Celsius used all of them without any of the safeguards those words normally imply. There's a simple rule that this story reinforces: anyone offering guaranteed double-digit yields on your savings is either taking risks you don't fully understand — or risks they don't fully understand. Either way, the risk is yours. Ask specifically: what is my money doing right now? Who has audited that? What happens if it goes wrong? If the answer is vague, so is your money."

**[OUTRO — 6:15–6:30]**
"1.7 million customers. $20 billion frozen. A CEO quietly selling his own tokens while recording weekly YouTube videos reassuring the people buying them. Subscribe — every week we find another story exactly like this one."
