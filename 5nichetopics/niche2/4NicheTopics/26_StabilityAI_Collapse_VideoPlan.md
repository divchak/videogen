## a) 🎬 YOUTUBE SCRIPT (Full Narration ~1,000 words)

**[HOOK — 0:00–0:30]**
"In 2022, Stability AI was the most exciting company in artificial intelligence. It had released Stable Diffusion — a free, open-source image generation model that anyone could download, run locally, and modify freely. It was the most democratizing release in AI history to that point. Investors valued the company at $1 billion. Then came the unpaid cloud bills. The mass exodus of key researchers. The CEO who had fabricated credentials on his LinkedIn profile. And a company that had given away its most valuable asset for free — and then couldn't figure out how to make money from the goodwill it generated."

**[BACKGROUND — 0:30–1:30]**
"Stability AI was founded by Emad Mostaque, a British-Bangladeshi former hedge fund analyst who had an infectious, almost evangelical enthusiasm for the idea that AI should be open, decentralized, and accessible to everyone — not locked behind API paywalls at large corporations. He raised $101 million in 2022 and used it partly to fund the development and open-source release of Stable Diffusion.

The release of Stable Diffusion in August 2022 was a cultural moment in AI. The model was downloaded millions of times within weeks. An entire ecosystem — tools, apps, artists, businesses, fine-tuned variants — grew around it organically. Stability AI became the reference point for what open-source AI could look like. Emad Mostaque was everywhere: conferences, podcasts, government meetings, speaking about AI's potential to solve everything from PTSD to global poverty."

**[THE BUSINESS MODEL PROBLEM — 1:30–2:30]**
"But open-source AI has a fundamental commercial challenge that Stability AI never fully solved. Stable Diffusion was the company's most valuable asset — and it was free. Anyone could download it, run it, build products on top of it, charge for those products, and owe Stability nothing. Competitors could take the model and build competing products. The goodwill was enormous. The revenue was not.

Stability AI's commercial products — API access, enterprise licensing, a hosted product called DreamStudio — struggled to generate revenue proportional to the company's costs. Those costs were primarily GPU compute — renting the enormous computational resources needed to train and run AI models at scale. Reports from late 2023 suggested Stability AI's cloud computing bills, primarily from Amazon Web Services, had accumulated to the point where the company had fallen behind on payments. A company at the center of the AI revolution couldn't pay its cloud bill."

**[LEADERSHIP CRISIS — 2:30–3:30]**
"Doubts about Emad Mostaque's leadership and honesty began accumulating publicly. He had claimed on his LinkedIn profile to have degrees from Oxford that he did not hold — a fabrication that, when exposed, damaged his credibility significantly. His public statements about Stability AI's business included projections and claims that employees and investors found difficult to reconcile with internal realities.

Through 2023 and into 2024, the company experienced a sustained exodus of senior talent. The Head of Audio, Head of Research, and multiple senior engineers and executives departed in rapid succession. Former employees described internal chaos — unclear strategy, poor resource allocation, and a founder who was extraordinarily effective at telling compelling stories to the outside world but struggling to translate those stories into operational clarity inside the company."

**[MOSTAQUE RESIGNATION — 3:30–4:15]**
"In March 2024, Emad Mostaque resigned as CEO of Stability AI. He framed the departure as stepping aside to pursue 'decentralized AI' — an ideological position that sounded more like a graceful exit than a strategic choice. The board appointed interim co-CEOs from within the company while searching for permanent leadership.

Stability AI entered a restructuring phase. It laid off significant portions of its workforce. It sold Clipdrop — a popular AI photo editing tool — to Freepik for an undisclosed amount. The company that had defined open-source generative AI was now smaller, quieter, and eclipsed. OpenAI, Midjourney, Google, and Anthropic had all built sustainable commercial models around AI. Stability AI had built community — and community doesn't pay server bills."

**[LESSONS — 4:15–5:00]**
"Stability AI's story is one of ideology and economics in direct conflict. The open-source vision was genuine and impactful. Stable Diffusion changed how the world thought about generative AI. But a mission to democratize AI does not automatically come with a business model to sustain the mission. Giving away your core technology for free can build a platform — but only if you've designed a commercial layer on top of it that converts platform users into paying customers. Stability never built that layer before running out of runway to build it."

**[OUTRO — 5:00–5:15]**
"A $1 billion valuation. A model downloaded millions of times. Unpaid cloud bills. A CEO who fabricated his credentials. And the most impactful open-source AI release of the decade — without a viable business around it. Subscribe — we cover the gap between changing the world and making money."
