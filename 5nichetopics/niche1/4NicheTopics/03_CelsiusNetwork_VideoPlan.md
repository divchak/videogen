## a) 🎬 YOUTUBE SCRIPT (~900 words)

**[HOOK — 0:00–0:30]**
"Imagine waking up one morning and discovering your bank had locked all your money — with no timeline for when you'd get it back. That's exactly what happened to over 1.7 million customers of Celsius Network in June 2022. Their 'crypto bank' had frozen all withdrawals. The CEO had vanished from public view. And behind closed doors, the company was insolvent."

**[BACKGROUND — 0:30–1:30]**
"Celsius Network was founded in 2017 by Alex Mashinsky, a serial entrepreneur who had previously worked on Voice over IP technology. His pitch was simple and powerful: the big banks earn 20-30% on your deposits but only give you 0.5% back. Celsius would cut out the middleman and pass those returns directly to you — up to 18% annual yield.

It sounded revolutionary. Users flocked in. By 2022, Celsius had over $20 billion in assets under management and 1.7 million customers worldwide. Mashinsky called himself 'the CEO who put his money where his mouth is' — he appeared on weekly YouTube streams to answer user questions directly."

**[HOW IT ACTUALLY WORKED — 1:30–2:30]**
"But how did Celsius actually generate those high returns? It deployed user deposits into high-risk DeFi protocols, lending markets, and crypto yield strategies. It made concentrated bets on illiquid assets like stETH — a derivative of Ethereum that couldn't easily be converted back to regular ETH.

The riskiest part? Celsius was operating without the regulatory oversight of a bank. No FDIC insurance. No reserve requirements. No independent auditor. It was structured like a hedge fund but marketed like a savings account."

**[THE COLLAPSE — 2:30–3:45]**
"When the crypto market crashed in May 2022, Celsius began struggling with liquidity. Its stETH holdings couldn't be easily sold. Losses mounted from bad loans and failed strategies. On June 12, 2022, with no public warning, Celsius announced it was pausing all withdrawals, swaps, and transfers — citing 'extreme market conditions.'

For 1.7 million customers, this was catastrophic. Many had their entire net worth locked inside. Weeks later, in July 2022, Celsius filed for bankruptcy. Internal documents leaked to the press showed the company had a $1.2 billion hole in its balance sheet. Court filings revealed that just weeks before the freeze, executives had quietly withdrawn millions of their own funds."

**[MASHINSKY — FRAUD EXPOSED — 3:45–4:45]**
"Alex Mashinsky was not just a bad risk manager. According to federal prosecutors, he was a fraudster. In 2023, Mashinsky was arrested and charged with securities fraud, commodities fraud, and market manipulation. The indictment alleged that he had secretly sold $68 million worth of his own CEL tokens while telling customers publicly that Celsius was healthy and that they should buy more.

He had also, according to prosecutors, manipulated the price of CEL tokens artificially — creating the illusion of a healthy company — while the business was quietly dying."

**[CUSTOMER RECOVERY — 4:45–5:30]**
"The bankruptcy proceedings dragged on for over a year. In 2024, a recovery plan was approved — customers would receive roughly 67–72 cents on the dollar for their deposits, a partial but painful recovery. For those who had deposited at the peak of crypto prices, even 72 cents back meant significant real-world losses."

**[LESSONS — 5:30–6:15]**
"Celsius exposes what happens when a company uses the language of banking — 'deposits,' 'yield,' 'savings' — without any of the protections that make banking safe. When someone promises 18% guaranteed yield on your savings, ask one question: Who is taking the other side of that risk? Because somewhere, someone always is. And in Celsius's case, it was you."

**[OUTRO — 6:15–6:30]**
"1.7 million customers. $20 billion frozen. And a CEO quietly selling his own tokens while telling you to buy more. Subscribe for more — we document the collapses that should have been front-page news."
