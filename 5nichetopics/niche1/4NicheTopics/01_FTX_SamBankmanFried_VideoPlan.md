## a) 🎬 YOUTUBE SCRIPT (Full Narration ~950 words)

**[HOOK — 0:00–0:30]**
"In November 2022, the world's second-largest crypto exchange — worth $32 billion — collapsed completely in 72 hours. Millions of users tried to log in and withdraw their money. They couldn't. The founder, once called 'the next Warren Buffett,' was gone. This is the story of FTX and Sam Bankman-Fried."

**[BACKGROUND — 0:30–1:30]**
"Sam Bankman-Fried, or SBF as he was known online, looked like no ordinary billionaire. He drove a Toyota Corolla, lived with roommates in the Bahamas, slept on a beanbag at the office. He preached a philosophy called 'Effective Altruism' — make as much money as possible, give it all away. Tech media loved him. Politicians posed for photos with him. He donated to both political parties. He even testified before the US Congress as the voice of responsible crypto.

By 2021, FTX was processing over $15 billion in daily trading volume. SBF graced the covers of Forbes and Fortune. His net worth was estimated at $26 billion. He was 30 years old."

**[THE CRACKS APPEAR — 1:30–2:45]**
"But on November 2nd, 2022, a report by CoinDesk changed everything. It revealed that Alameda Research — SBF's private trading firm — held most of its assets in FTT, FTX's own token. In simple terms: FTX's balance sheet was built on its own made-up money.

Rival exchange Binance, which held $580 million worth of FTT, announced it would sell all of it. That single tweet triggered a bank run. In 24 hours, users tried to withdraw $6 billion from FTX. The exchange froze withdrawals. The FTT token crashed 80% in two days."

**[THE COLLAPSE — 2:45–4:00]**
"Binance briefly offered to buy FTX to bail it out. Then, after reviewing FTX's books — which reportedly showed a secret $8 billion hole — Binance walked away in hours. SBF desperately texted investors, VCs, anyone. No one came. On November 11, 2022, FTX filed for bankruptcy. SBF resigned. Over one million customers were left locked out of their funds.

Behind the scenes, investigators discovered that FTX had been secretly lending customer deposits to Alameda Research — its sister company — to fund risky bets, political donations, luxury real estate purchases in the Bahamas, and celebrity endorsements. This wasn't bad luck. It was fraud."

**[AFTERMATH & TRIAL — 4:00–5:15]**
"SBF was arrested in the Bahamas in December 2022. He was extradited to the United States and went to trial in October 2023. Former colleagues, including his ex-girlfriend Caroline Ellison who ran Alameda, testified against him. In November 2023, Sam Bankman-Fried was found guilty on all seven counts of fraud and conspiracy. In March 2024, he was sentenced to 25 years in federal prison.

The total customer losses? Estimated between $8 and $10 billion. Some creditors have since recovered partial funds through the bankruptcy process, but for many ordinary investors, it was life savings — gone."

**[LESSON — 5:15–6:00]**
"The FTX story is not just about crypto. It's about what happens when we confuse charisma for competence, and PR for accountability. SBF's hoodie, his 'altruism,' his media appearances — they were all a brand. The lesson: no matter how trustworthy someone looks, always ask: where is the actual money? Who is auditing the books? Is this regulated?

Because the next FTX won't look like a sketchy operation. It'll look exactly like FTX did — friendly, trendy, and endorsed by everyone."

**[OUTRO — 6:00–6:30]**
"FTX. $32 billion. Gone in 72 hours. If you want to see how the next startup fraud is being built right now, hit subscribe — we cover these stories before they become headlines."
