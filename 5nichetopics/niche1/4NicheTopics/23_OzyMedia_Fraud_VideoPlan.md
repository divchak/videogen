## a) 🎬 YOUTUBE SCRIPT (~880 words)

**[HOOK — 0:00–0:30]**
"It ended on a Zoom call. On a call with Goldman Sachs executives, a man pretending to be a YouTube executive told them that Ozy Media — a digital news startup valued at hundreds of millions — had 250 million monthly users. The voice wasn't from YouTube. It was Ozy's own co-founder, doing an impersonation. That call led to one of the most bizarre corporate fraud cases in media history."

**[BACKGROUND — 0:30–1:30]**
"Ozy Media was founded in 2013 by Carlos Watson, a former Goldman Sachs banker and CNN anchor, and co-founder Samir Rao. Their pitch was a digital news brand targeting 'the change generation' — millennials who wanted global, diverse stories beyond the mainstream media bubble.

Ozy built a slick brand. A popular newsletter. A YouTube channel. A live festival. Watson became a regular guest on major news networks. The company raised tens of millions from investors. Watson spoke at the World Economic Forum in Davos. Ozy seemed like the future of millennial media."

**[THE PHONE CALL — 1:30–2:30]**
"In September 2021, the New York Times published a bombshell investigation. It revealed that in February 2021, Ozy's co-founder Samir Rao had participated in a Zoom call with Goldman Sachs executives who were considering investing in Ozy. During the call, Rao impersonated a YouTube executive — faking the voice of someone from YouTube's partnership team — to fraudulently vouch for Ozy's business performance and audience metrics.

When one Goldman executive became suspicious and called YouTube directly, YouTube confirmed it had no involvement. Goldman reported the incident to federal authorities."

**[THE FAKE METRICS — 2:30–3:15]**
"The New York Times investigation went deeper. It revealed that Ozy's claimed audience figures — including the 250 million monthly unique users — appeared to be significantly inflated. Ozy was claiming viewer counts that independent analytics couldn't corroborate. Former Ozy employees told the Times they had been pressured to report overstated metrics."

**[THE IMPLOSION — 3:15–4:00]**
"The article published on September 26, 2021 was an extinction-level event for Ozy. Its board members — including former government officials — began resigning within 24 hours. Watson attempted damage control, calling the Goldman call 'an isolated incident' and describing Rao as having a 'mental health crisis.'

On September 29, 2021 — three days after the article — Ozy shut down. Then, in a surreal move, Watson announced a 'revival' two weeks later, attempting to restart Ozy with himself in charge. Investors and former board members publicly denounced the effort."

**[WATSON ARREST — 4:00–4:30]**
"In October 2023, Carlos Watson was arrested by federal agents and charged with securities fraud, wire fraud, and multiple related charges. Prosecutors alleged that Watson had orchestrated a multi-year scheme to defraud investors with false financial metrics and fabricated business relationships."

**[LESSONS — 4:30–5:15]**
"Ozy is a lesson in how media metrics are easily manipulated and how charisma can blind investors to basic due diligence. Carlos Watson was persuasive, connected, and understood how to build the right relationships. But no one verified the numbers. And when a single phone call exposed the lie, the entire edifice collapsed in three days.

Always verify. Not just the vision — the numbers."

**[OUTRO — 5:15–5:30]**
"One Zoom call. Three days to collapse. And a co-founder doing a voice impersonation of a YouTube executive. Subscribe — we find the stories that write themselves."
