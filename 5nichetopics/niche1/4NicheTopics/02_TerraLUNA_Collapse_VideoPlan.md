## a) 🎬 YOUTUBE SCRIPT (Full Narration ~950 words)

**[HOOK — 0:00–0:30]**
"In May 2022, $60 billion in crypto value disappeared in four days. Not stolen. Not hacked. Just… gone. Evaporated into code. This was the Terra/LUNA collapse — the largest single financial implosion in cryptocurrency history, and almost no one saw it coming."

**[BACKGROUND — 0:30–1:30]**
"Terra was a blockchain project built by a South Korean developer named Do Kwon. Its big innovation was an 'algorithmic stablecoin' called UST — a digital dollar that wasn't backed by actual dollars. Instead, it used a complex mathematical system involving a second token called LUNA to maintain its $1 peg.

The system worked like this: if UST fell below $1, you could burn UST to mint LUNA, reducing supply and pushing the price back up. If UST went above $1, the reverse happened. It was elegant on paper. Do Kwon called critics 'poor.' He publicly posted about how 95% of crypto projects would fail — just not his."

**[THE ANCHOR — ANCHOR PROTOCOL — 1:30–2:30]**
"The fuel behind UST's explosive growth was a platform called Anchor Protocol. It promised 20% annual yield on UST deposits — in a world where banks gave 0.5%. Billions poured in. At its peak, Anchor held over $17 billion in UST deposits. Retail investors from the US, Korea, India — ordinary people — treated it like a savings account.

But that 20% yield was not sustainable. It was being subsidized out of the project's own reserves. It was essentially a time bomb dressed up as a savings account."

**[THE ATTACK — 2:30–3:45]**
"In early May 2022, someone — identity still unknown — began withdrawing massive amounts of UST from Anchor and simultaneously dumping it on exchanges. The scale was enormous. UST began to lose its peg, dipping to $0.98, then $0.95. The algorithm kicked in, minting billions of new LUNA tokens to compensate. But the selling pressure was overwhelming.

In a death spiral, more LUNA was printed, LUNA's price collapsed, which devalued the collateral, which put more pressure on UST — which needed more LUNA minted. In 72 hours, LUNA went from $80 to $0.0002. UST fell to $0.10. $60 billion in combined market cap was erased."

**[THE HUMAN COST — 3:45–4:45]**
"This wasn't just numbers on a screen. Korean news reported suicides. Online forums filled with people describing life savings wiped out. A nurse in the US lost $50,000. A retiree in Canada lost his pension substitute. Do Kwon himself had marketed UST as a 'savings account for the unbanked.' The people who lost the most were those who could afford it least.

Do Kwon initially tweeted that he was 'not worried.' He proposed a revival plan that failed. Then he went quiet. Then he disappeared."

**[DO KWON — FUGITIVE — 4:45–5:30]**
"South Korean authorities issued an arrest warrant for Do Kwon in September 2022. Interpol issued a Red Notice — the closest thing to an international arrest warrant. For months, Do Kwon taunted on Twitter, insisting he was 'not on the run.' In March 2023, he was arrested in Montenegro trying to travel on falsified documents. In 2024, he was extradited to the United States to face fraud charges."

**[LESSONS — 5:30–6:15]**
"The Terra collapse exposed a critical truth: if something promises 20% guaranteed returns, it is not a savings account. It is a risk. The word 'stable' in stablecoin means nothing unless it is backed by real assets. Algorithmic systems can spiral in ways their creators never fully modeled. And charismatic founders who mock critics are often the most dangerous ones to trust."

**[OUTRO — 6:15–6:45]**
"Sixty billion dollars. Four days. And a founder who called you poor for questioning him. Follow for more startup post-mortems — we cover the collapses that changed the world."
