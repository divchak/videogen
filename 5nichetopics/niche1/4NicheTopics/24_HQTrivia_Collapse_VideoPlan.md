## a) 🎬 YOUTUBE SCRIPT (~820 words)

**[HOOK — 0:00–0:30]**
"At 3pm and 9pm every day in 2018, millions of people stopped what they were doing, opened an app, and tried to win cash by answering 12 trivia questions. HQ Trivia was the first app to create a truly live, shared cultural moment on mobile. At its peak, 2.3 million people played simultaneously. Then, 18 months later, it was gone."

**[BACKGROUND — 0:30–1:30]**
"HQ Trivia was created by Rus Yusupov and Colin Kroll, the co-founders who had previously built Vine — the six-second video app that Twitter had acquired and then killed. After Vine's death, they took what they'd learned about real-time mobile engagement and built something entirely new.

The concept: a live trivia game show, twice a day, hosted by a real person, with real cash prizes. Answer all 12 questions correctly, split the prize pool. It launched in August 2017 and grew explosively through word of mouth. Demi Lovato guest-hosted. The Oscars ran an HQ game. Tech Twitter could talk about nothing else."

**[PEAK MOMENT — 1:30–2:15]**
"On January 14, 2018 — Super Bowl eve — HQ Trivia had 2.38 million simultaneous players. It was a genuine cultural event. The host, Scott Rogowsky, became an internet celebrity. HQ Trivia was valued at $100 million. VC firms invested. Every media company discussed adding a live trivia element. Snapchat launched a copy. YouTube launched a copy."

**[THE PROBLEMS — 2:15–3:15]**
"But HQ Trivia had serious structural problems. The cash prize pool — typically $2,500 to $5,000 — was split among sometimes hundreds of winners, meaning individual winners received cents. The incentive to keep playing dropped sharply.

Server crashes during peak games — when millions logged in simultaneously — became regular occurrences. Users encountered frustrating technical failures at critical moments. Aggressive question difficulty spikes in later rounds felt unfair.

The CEO and co-founder Colin Kroll died of an accidental drug overdose in December 2018 — a devastating personal loss that disrupted leadership at a critical time."

**[THE DECLINE — 3:15–4:00]**
"In 2019, HQ Trivia struggled to sustain engagement. The novelty had worn off. User numbers dropped from millions to hundreds of thousands. The company raised money but struggled to find a sustainable business model. It experimented with sports trivia, word games, and subscription features — none achieved traction.

In February 2020, HQ Trivia's backer Intermedia Labs announced it was shutting down the app — the company had run out of money. The shutdown happened within days of the announcement. Staff were let go immediately."

**[LESSONS — 4:00–4:50]**
"HQ Trivia created something genuinely new: a real-time, shared cultural moment on mobile. But novelty fades. Without a sustainable monetization model — advertising, subscription, a prize structure that created real aspiration — the app was always burning through cash without a viable long-term engine.

It also demonstrated how fragile live apps are: any technical failure destroys the experience irreparably. The lesson: a great mechanic is not a business model."

**[OUTRO — 4:50–5:05]**
"2.3 million simultaneous players. Gone in 18 months. Do you remember your username? Subscribe for more digital nostalgia post-mortems."
