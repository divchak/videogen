## a) 🎬 YOUTUBE SCRIPT (~920 words)

**[HOOK — 0:00–0:30]**
"Two men borrowed billions of dollars from hundreds of companies — then disappeared on a yacht. Three Arrows Capital was the most influential crypto hedge fund in the world. And then, in June 2022, it simply ceased to exist — leaving a trail of devastated lenders and a $3.5 billion hole in the market."

**[BACKGROUND — 0:30–1:30]**
"Three Arrows Capital, or 3AC, was founded in 2012 by two Singaporean childhood friends — Su Zhu and Kyle Davies. They started as forex traders and gradually migrated into crypto. By 2021, 3AC was one of the most respected names in crypto investing. They were early investors in Ethereum, Avalanche, and dozens of other projects that turned huge profits.

At their peak, 3AC managed an estimated $18 billion in assets. They weren't just investors — they were kingmakers. If 3AC backed your project, it was legitimized. Their Twitter presence was authoritative. They were invited to all the major conferences. Every lender wanted to be their partner."

**[THE STRATEGY — 1:30–2:30]**
"3AC's strategy was essentially: borrow as much as possible, invest in crypto assets, and ride the bull market. They had credit lines with nearly every major crypto lender — Voyager Digital, BlockFi, Genesis, Babel Finance — borrowing hundreds of millions from each without any of them knowing the full picture of 3AC's total borrowings.

This is called counterparty risk — when you lend to someone without knowing how much they already owe to others. In traditional finance, credit checks prevent this. In crypto in 2021, due diligence was practically non-existent. Everyone trusted 3AC's reputation."

**[THE TERRA EXPOSURE — 2:30–3:30]**
"When Terra/LUNA collapsed in May 2022, 3AC was severely exposed. They had invested over $200 million in LUNA, which went to near zero in four days. But their problems ran deeper. 3AC had taken massive leveraged positions across crypto markets. As prices fell, their collateral was being liquidated from every direction.

The firm faced margin calls — demands to repay loans or post more collateral — from lenders simultaneously. They had no liquidity to meet them. Behind the scenes, Su Zhu and Kyle Davies were no longer answering emails or phone calls."

**[THE GHOST FOUNDERS — 3:30–4:30]**
"By late June 2022, lenders began publicly disclosing that 3AC had defaulted on hundreds of millions in loans. Voyager Digital disclosed a $650 million exposure and later filed for bankruptcy. Genesis Capital disclosed $2.36 billion in claims. BlockFi was forced into a deal with FTX — which itself later collapsed.

On June 27, 2022, a British Virgin Islands court ordered 3AC into liquidation. Su Zhu and Kyle Davies were nowhere to be found. They had left Singapore. They were not responding to court-ordered summons. Bankruptcy liquidators literally could not find them."

**[THE YACHT — 4:30–5:15]**
"It later emerged that around the time of their fund's collapse, 3AC had placed an order for a $50 million luxury superyacht — named 'Much Wow' — a Dogecoin meme reference. The yacht order was placed even as the fund was imploding and borrowers were losing billions.

Su Zhu was eventually arrested in September 2023 in Singapore while trying to board a flight. He served time in prison. Kyle Davies remained a fugitive, giving occasional media interviews from an undisclosed location."

**[LESSONS — 5:15–6:00]**
"3AC is a masterclass in how leverage and reputation can disguise catastrophic risk. Every lender assumed someone else had done the due diligence. Everyone assumed 3AC was too big to fail. No single lender had the full picture. And by the time anyone understood the total exposure, it was too late.

In crypto's boom years, reputation replaced regulation. The results were predictable."

**[OUTRO — 6:00–6:30]**
"18 billion in assets. Gone. A $50 million yacht order placed during the collapse. And founders who vanished into thin air. Subscribe — we cover the stories that the hype machine buried."
