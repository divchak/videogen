## a) 🎬 YOUTUBE SCRIPT (Full Narration ~1,000 words)

**[HOOK — 0:00–0:30]**
"It ended on a Zoom call. During a meeting with Goldman Sachs executives, someone representing Ozy Media impersonated a YouTube executive — doing a voice impression — to falsely endorse the company's business metrics. The man doing the impression was Ozy's own co-founder. That one call triggered an investigation that revealed nearly everything Ozy had been telling its investors was either exaggerated or fabricated. Three days after the New York Times published the story, the company was gone. This is one of the most bizarre corporate collapses in media history — and it contains a critical lesson about charisma and due diligence."

**[BACKGROUND — 0:30–1:30]**
"Ozy Media was founded in 2013 by Carlos Watson, a former Goldman Sachs banker and CNN anchor who brought exceptional credibility and polish to the company. The brand aimed at the 'change generation' — millennials who wanted global, diverse, forward-looking stories that mainstream media wasn't covering. They launched a newsletter, a YouTube channel, live festivals, and a website. Watson became a fixture at Davos, a regular guest on CNN and MSNBC, a recognizable face in the space where media, tech, and politics intersect.

By 2021, Ozy had raised tens of millions from investors. Watson had cultivated relationships with former government officials, prominent figures in media and tech, and institutional backers who saw Ozy as the vanguard of a new kind of media company. The brand felt legitimate because Watson made it feel legitimate. He was masterful at creating presence."

**[THE PHONE CALL — 1:30–2:30]**
"In February 2021, Ozy co-founder Samir Rao was on a Zoom call with Goldman Sachs executives who were evaluating a potential investment. During that call, Rao impersonated a YouTube executive — using a disguised voice — to provide a fraudulent endorsement of Ozy's viewership numbers and business performance. He was claiming to be a representative from YouTube's partnership team vouching for Ozy's audience metrics.

One of the Goldman executives grew suspicious during the call and afterward called YouTube directly. YouTube confirmed they had no knowledge of the call and had sent no representative. Goldman immediately reported the incident to federal authorities. The call that was supposed to close a deal instead opened a criminal investigation."

**[THE FAKE METRICS — 2:30–3:15]**
"The New York Times investigation that followed went deeper than the Zoom call. It found that Ozy's claimed audience figures — including a claimed 250 million monthly unique users — appeared to be significantly inflated. Independent analytics tools showed substantially lower numbers. Former Ozy employees told the Times they had been pressured to report metrics that didn't reflect reality. The company's business relationships — which Watson had described in pitches to investors — were not what he had represented them to be.

The entire valuation thesis for Ozy depended on a large, growing, engaged audience. That audience, to the extent it existed at all as claimed, was substantially manufactured."

**[THE IMPLOSION — 3:15–4:00]**
"The New York Times article published on September 26, 2021 was devastating. Within 24 hours, Ozy's board began resigning en masse — members who included prominent former government officials and respected executives who apparently had no idea what had been happening inside the company. Watson attempted damage control, calling the Goldman call 'an isolated incident' and describing Rao as having suffered a 'mental health crisis.'

On September 29 — three days after publication — Ozy shut down. Watson announced the closure publicly. Then, in a move that was simultaneously audacious and inadvisable, he announced a company 'revival' two weeks later, attempting to rebuild Ozy with himself still in charge. Investors and former board members publicly rejected the effort."

**[WATSON ARREST — 4:00–4:30]**
"In October 2023, Carlos Watson was arrested by federal agents and charged with securities fraud, wire fraud, and conspiracy. The indictment described a multi-year scheme to defraud investors through false statements about audience metrics, business relationships, and financial performance. The government alleged that Watson had orchestrated the deceptions at the highest level — that the Zoom call was not an isolated incident but part of a systematic pattern."

**[LESSONS — 4:30–5:15]**
"Ozy is a masterclass in what happens when charisma runs too far ahead of verification. Carlos Watson was genuinely talented — an engaging broadcaster, a skilled relationship builder, a credible media personality. Investors were dazzled by his access and his story. Nobody independently verified the numbers. Nobody stress-tested the audience metrics against third-party tools. The lesson: in media, audience metrics are easily inflated and cheaply purchased. Verification is a one-hour job. Due diligence should always include asking: can I verify this number myself, independently, right now?"

**[OUTRO — 5:15–5:30]**
"A voice impression on a Zoom call. Three days from article to shutdown. A founder arrested on federal fraud charges. And a company that apparently had a fraction of the audience it claimed. Subscribe — and always verify the numbers yourself."
