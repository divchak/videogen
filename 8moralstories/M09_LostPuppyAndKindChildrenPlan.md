# 🐾 M09 — "The Lost Puppy and the Kind Street"
### Kids Moral Story | British Setting | Pixar 3D Animation | Narration Only
### Moral: **Help others without expecting anything in return**
### Duration: ~4 Minutes | Age: 3–8 | Comedy Level: ⭐⭐⭐⭐

---

## 📖 STORY SUMMARY

Rosie and Pip are two children walking home from school when they find a tiny, wet, shivering puppy sitting alone on a London street in the rain. They help the puppy — despite knowing it will make them late and muddy — and go door to door in their neighbourhood to find its owner. One grumpy old neighbour, Mr. Cratchley, who never speaks to anyone, turns out to be the owner. When his puppy Pebbles runs in and licks his face, the grumpy man breaks into the first smile anyone has seen from him in years. The unexpected joy of their kindness transforms the whole street.

## 🎬 SCENE-BY-SCENE PLAN

### 🎬 SCENE 1 — After School in the Rain [0:00–0:25]
**Setting:** A rainy London afternoon — grey skies, wet pavements, red double-decker buses in the background, children in uniforms running home.

**Narration (English):**
> *On a Tuesday afternoon in October, when the rain was falling in the particularly determined way that only British rain can manage, Rosie and Pip were walking home from school under one Union Jack umbrella that was slightly too small for both of them. They were talking about what to have for tea. And then Rosie stopped.*

**Scene Description:**
- Rosie and Pip walking under a tilting umbrella — both getting half-wet
- Rosie suddenly stops — Pip walks into her
- They both look down

---

### 🎬 SCENE 2 — Finding Pebbles [0:25–0:55]
**Setting:** Street corner — a tiny sodden puppy huddled against a postbox.

**Narration (English):**
> *There, sitting against the old red postbox, was the smallest, wettest, most thoroughly miserable puppy Rosie had ever seen. It was grey and round and soaking and shivering. It looked up at them with enormous dark eyes — the eyes of someone who very much hoped someone might care. Rosie immediately crouched down. Pip stood back. "We'll be late," said Pip. "And muddy," he added. Rosie looked up at him. "Yes," she said. "We will."*

**Scene Description:**
- Puppy revealed — soaking wet, trembling, enormous eyes
- Rosie crouches — puppy looks at her — small wag of tail
- Pip looks at his school uniform, looks at the puppy, looks at Rosie's determined face
- Pip sighs, puts down his enormous bag, crouches too
- **COMEDY:** The bag falls open and approximately forty things fall out of it onto the wet pavement

---

### 🎬 SCENE 3 — Helping the Puppy [0:55–1:20]
**Setting:** Under the umbrella — the children wrap Pebbles in Pip's school jumper.

**Narration (English):**
> *Rosie wrapped the puppy in Pip's school jumper — which Pip was not entirely delighted about, but did not argue — and held her close under the umbrella. The puppy stopped shivering. Her tail began to wag in tiny uncertain wags. Rosie checked the collar: a small brass tag with one word: "Pebbles." The puppy's name. But no address. No phone number. Just Pebbles.*

**Scene Description:**
- Rosie wrapping puppy in Pip's jumper — Pip watches his jumper disappear
- Puppy's tail begins wagging — tiny uncertain wags that get bigger
- Rosie reads the tag: "Pebbles"
- Pip looks at his cold arms, then at the puppy, then can't help smiling

---

### 🎬 SCENE 4 — Door to Door [1:20–1:55]
**Setting:** The children go door to door on the street — various houses.

**Narration (English):**
> *They knocked on eight doors. The first family had a cat and couldn't help. The second man was in the bath. The third woman was on the telephone and gave them a biscuit anyway. The fourth door had no answer. The fifth family had three children who all tried to adopt the puppy immediately, which was not helpful. The sixth and seventh also didn't know. At the eighth door — number fourteen, with the always-closed curtains — they hesitated. That was Mr. Cratchley's house. Nobody knocked on Mr. Cratchley's door.*

**Scene Description:**
- Montage of doors — each with a funny reaction
- Door 2: man at door in bathrobe with soapy head — "Sorry! Bath!" 
- Door 3: biscuit handed out mid-phone-call
- Door 5: three kids all grabbing for Pebbles — arms everywhere
- Rosie and Pip standing at number 14 — curtains with a small gap, an eye visible
- **COMEDY:** Pebbles wriggles out and goes to Number 14 herself — scratches at the door with her tiny paw

---

### 🎬 SCENE 5 — Mr. Cratchley's Door [1:55–2:25]
**Setting:** Number 14 — the door opens very slowly.

**Narration (English):**
> *The door opened. Mr. Cratchley stood there in his grey cardigan and slippers, looking at them with an expression that had forgotten how to be anything other than severe. He was about to say something. And then he looked down at the small grey bundle in Rosie's arms. His mouth opened. He said nothing for a very long moment. Then, in a voice that was rough from not being used enough: "Pebbles."*

**Scene Description:**
- Door opens slowly — Mr. Cratchley framed in doorway
- His face: closed, habitual sternness
- His eyes travel down to Pebbles
- Pebbles sees him — ears prick — tail goes WILD
- Mr. Cratchley's voice: "Pebbles" — whispered

---

### 🎬 SCENE 6 — The Reunion [2:25–2:55]
**Setting:** Doorstep — Pebbles in Rosie's arms, reaching toward Mr. Cratchley.

**Narration (English):**
> *Rosie held Pebbles out. The puppy launched herself with such enthusiasm that she hit Mr. Cratchley squarely in the face and licked every available centimetre of it in approximately four seconds. Mr. Cratchley stumbled backward. He caught Pebbles. He held her very tightly. And then — on a Tuesday in October on a rainy London street — Mr. Cratchley smiled. It was not a small smile. It was enormous. It changed his whole face. It made him look thirty years younger. Neither Rosie nor Pip had ever seen him smile before. They had never seen most of the neighbours smile quite like that either.*

**Scene Description:**
- Pebbles launches at Mr. Cratchley — face-lick attack
- Mr. Cratchley staggers back — clutches Pebbles — laughing
- The smile — close-up — transformative
- Neighbours have quietly come out of their doors to look
- **COMEDY:** Mr. Cratchley is now sitting on his hallway floor, being thoroughly licked, laughing helplessly

---

### 🎬 SCENE 7 — The Unexpected Gift [2:55–3:20]
**Setting:** Doorstep — Mr. Cratchley stands again, composed but different.

**Narration (English):**
> *Mr. Cratchley stood up, straightened his cardigan, and looked at Rosie and Pip. He said that Pebbles had been missing since that morning and he had been out of his mind with worry. He asked how they knew to come to his door. They said they hadn't — they had just knocked on every door until they found the right one. Mr. Cratchley nodded for a long time. Then he went inside and came back with a tin of shortbread that had never been opened, and a thank-you that was so genuine and so quiet that both children stood very still when they heard it.*

**Scene Description:**
- Mr. Cratchley handing them shortbread tin
- He says "thank you" — simple, direct — children can see how much it means
- Pebbles pokes her head out from Mr. Cratchley's arms and gives a tiny wag

---

### 🎬 SCENE 8 — Street Transformation [3:20–3:45]
**Setting:** The street — neighbours have emerged, the rain has lightened.

**Narration (English):**
> *Something strange and wonderful happened on Bramblewick Street that Tuesday. The neighbours who had come out to look found themselves talking to each other for the first time in months. Mr. Cratchley stood at his gate with Pebbles and actually waved at someone passing. And Rosie and Pip walked home — late, muddy, one of them without a jumper — and they felt, without quite being able to explain why, completely full.*

**Scene Description:**
- Neighbours chatting in the street — the rain lightening
- Mr. Cratchley at his gate, Pebbles in his arms, waving
- Rosie and Pip walking home — muddy, happy, glowing
- Pip hugging his enormous empty bag — his jumper still on Pebbles in the background

---

### 🎬 SCENE 9 — Moral Moment [3:45–4:00]
**Narration (English):**
> *Remember: Sometimes the greatest reward for kindness is not something you receive. It is something you feel.*

**Scene Description:**
- Card: **"Help others simply because you can"**
- Pebbles waves a tiny paw at camera from Mr. Cratchley's arms
