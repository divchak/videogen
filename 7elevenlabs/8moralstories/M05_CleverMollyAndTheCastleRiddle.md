# 🧩 M05 — "Clever Molly and the Castle Riddle"
### Kids Moral Story | British Setting | Pixar 3D Animation | Narration Only
### Moral: **Brains beat brawn every time — think before you act**
### Duration: ~4 Minutes | Age: 3–8 | Comedy Level: ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐

---

## 📖 STORY SUMMARY

Three big, strong, confident knights are trapped in the castle because a grumpy troll has locked the gate with a riddle they cannot solve. Young Molly, the smallest person in the village, is passing by and stops to listen. She solves the riddle instantly. The troll is amazed and lets everyone in. The knights cannot believe that a little girl solved what they could not — and Molly teaches them that thinking carefully is the greatest strength of all.

---

## 🎭 CHARACTER PROFILES

| Character | Description | Personality | British Detail |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Molly** | 8-year-old girl, wild red hair, green dungarees, always carrying a book | Curious, calm, logical, loves puzzles | Has a notebook where she writes down interesting things |
| **The Troll** | Short, bumpy-nosed troll sitting on a stone under the gate | Actually quite shy — asks riddles because he loves them, not to be mean | Has a very posh British accent despite being a troll |
| **Sir Crumpton** | Tallest knight, enormous shoulders | Very confident, always wrong first | Gives answers in a booming voice as if volume equals correctness |
| **Sir Wobbleton** | Medium knight, round belly | Copies whatever Crumpton says | Nods vigorously then looks confused |
| **Sir Nibbs** | Smallest of the three knights but still bigger than Molly | Tries to seem smart, fails | Carries a very large dictionary he has never opened |
| **Wise Owl** | Old owl on the castle parapet | Says nothing — just watches — occasionally slow-blinks | Has tiny spectacles |

## 🎬 SCENE-BY-SCENE PLAN

### 🎬 SCENE 1 — The Locked Castle [0:00–0:25]
**Setting:** A grand English castle with a big oak gate. Three large knights standing before it looking baffled. A small stone troll sits under the gate arch, arms folded.

**Narration (English):**
> *Oakvale Castle had stood for five hundred years without a single problem. Until the day that Bertram the Troll decided to sit under the gate and refuse to let anyone in until they answered his riddle. He had been sitting there for three days. And the three bravest, biggest, most armour-clad knights in the kingdom had been standing in front of him for three days — and not one of them had got the answer right.*

**Scene Description:**
- Wide shot of castle, knights standing outside looking defeated
- Troll sitting cross-armed under the arch, quite comfortable with a little camping chair and a thermos of tea
- **COMEDY:** One knight is asleep standing up — another nudges him and he snaps awake saying "I knew that!"

---

### 🎬 SCENE 2 — The Riddle [0:25–0:55]
**Setting:** Gate — troll recites the riddle.

**Narration (English):**
> *The troll recited his riddle in a surprisingly posh voice: "I have cities, but no houses live there. I have mountains, but no trees grow there. I have water, but no fish swim there. I have roads, but no people walk there. What am I?" Sir Crumpton immediately shouted: "A VERY LARGE SOUP!" Sir Wobbleton shouted: "ALSO A SOUP!" Sir Nibbs opened his enormous dictionary, looked at it upside down, and declared: "The ocean!"*

**Scene Description:**
- Troll recites riddle clearly, with a tiny bow
- Crumpton answers with absolute confidence: "A SOUP!" — Troll stares blankly
- Wobbleton copies: "ALSO SOUP!" — Troll buries his face in his hand
- Nibbs opens dictionary upside down — flips it, looks at a random page — says "The ocean!" proudly
- Wise Owl on the parapet slow-blinks

---

### 🎬 SCENE 3 — Molly Arrives [0:55–1:20]
**Setting:** Molly walks up the path, book under her arm.

**Narration (English):**
> *Just then, a small girl with wild red hair came walking up the path. She stopped to see what all the fuss was about. She listened to the riddle once. She thought for exactly four seconds. Then she said, very quietly: "A map." The troll sat up straight. His bumpy nose twitched. He looked at Molly. He looked at the knights. He looked back at Molly.*

**Scene Description:**
- Molly walks up, curious expression
- She stops, listens — finger taps chin — looks up in thought
- Four-second pause (clock ticks on screen)
- Molly: "A map." — said simply
- Troll's jaw drops open slowly
- **COMEDY:** The three knights all turn to look at each other with expressions of pure disbelief

---

### 🎬 SCENE 4 — The Troll's Reaction [1:20–1:50]
**Setting:** Gate — the troll stands up, slowly clapping.

**Narration (English):**
> *"CORRECT!" said the troll, jumping to his feet with more enthusiasm than expected for someone who had been sitting on a stone for three days. He grabbed Molly's hand and shook it vigorously. "In three days, nobody — NOBODY — got that right! How did you work it out so fast?" Molly said she had simply thought about what has cities but isn't a city, and immediately thought of a map. The troll looked as if this was the most wonderful thing he had ever heard.*

**Scene Description:**
- Troll leaps up, shakes Molly's hand with both his
- Molly explains calmly, pointing to her notebook
- Troll beaming — scribbles something in a little notebook of his own
- **COMEDY:** Sir Nibbs quietly opens his dictionary to "M" and finds "Map" — reads the definition — slaps the book shut and nods as if he knew all along

---

### 🎬 SCENE 5 — The Knights React [1:50–2:15]
**Setting:** Gate opens — all walk into the courtyard.

**Narration (English):**
> *The great gate swung open. The troll waved them all through. The three knights shuffled in, heads down. Sir Crumpton, who had never been wrong about anything in his life, looked at Molly with an expression that was partly embarrassed, partly impressed, and mostly very confused. "How," he said slowly, "did someone so small know something so big?" And Molly said simply: "I read."*

**Scene Description:**
- Knights shuffle through gate quietly
- Crumpton towers over Molly — looking down at her
- Molly holds up her book with a calm smile
- Wise Owl on the parapet gives one single slow approving nod

---

### 🎬 SCENE 6 — A Lesson from the Troll [2:15–2:45]
**Setting:** Courtyard — troll follows them in and sits down with everyone.

**Narration (English):**
> *The troll poured four cups of tea from his thermos — he had been hoping for company for three days — and explained that riddles were his favourite thing because they proved that thinking matters more than strength. "Any troll can block a gate," he said, "but it takes a very special person to open one with their mind." The knights looked at their enormous biceps. Then at Molly's small arms. Then at their enormous biceps again.*

**Scene Description:**
- Troll pours tea for everyone — Molly accepts, knights all take cups too despite themselves
- **COMEDY:** Troll pours tea — three knights all sitting on the ground awkwardly, armour clanking — all trying to drink tea in full armour

---

### 🎬 SCENE 7 — Molly Teaches the Knights [2:45–3:20]
**Setting:** Courtyard garden — Molly opens her book and the knights gather around.

**Narration (English):**
> *Molly spent the afternoon teaching the three knights how to solve riddles. Sir Crumpton was surprisingly good at them — once he stopped shouting his answers. Sir Wobbleton discovered he liked riddles even more than jousting. And Sir Nibbs — who had been carrying a dictionary for years without ever reading it — finally opened it to page one and began to read. The troll was so happy he made biscuits.*

**Scene Description:**
- Molly at the centre, knights gathered around — it looks like a school lesson in a meadow
- Crumpton gets one right — does a tiny celebration dance in full armour (very clanky)
- Nibbs reading dictionary — looks delighted at first page
- Troll arrives with a plate of biscuits on a tray

---

### 🎬 SCENE 8 — Moral Moment [3:20–3:35]
**Narration (English):**
> *And so it was that the smallest person in the village opened the biggest gate in the kingdom — with nothing but a good mind and a love of books. Remember: You do not need big muscles to solve big problems. You need a curious mind.*

**Scene Description:**
- Card: **"Think first — your brain is your greatest power"**
- Molly at the gate, tapping her head — troll gives a thumbs up from behind
