# ⚔️ M03 — "The Kind Knight Who Forgot His Sword"
### Kids Moral Story | British Setting | Pixar 3D Animation | Narration Only
### Moral: **True bravery is kindness**
### Duration: ~4 Minutes | Age: 3–8 | Comedy Level: ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐

---

## 📖 STORY SUMMARY

Sir Finn is the smallest knight in all of England. On the day he is supposed to face a terrifying dragon to prove his bravery, he forgets his sword at the castle! But rather than turning back or giving up, Finn meets the dragon — and instead of fighting, he notices the dragon has a thorn in its foot. He gently removes it. The dragon, who was only roaring because of the pain, becomes the gentlest creature in the kingdom. Sir Finn becomes a legend — not for his sword, but for his kindness.

---

## 🎭 CHARACTER PROFILES

| Character | Description | Personality | British Detail |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Sir Finn** | 7-year-old boy knight, tiny armour that's slightly too big, wobbly helmet | Nervous but warm-hearted, accidentally brave | His armour has a little dent where he walked into a doorframe |
| **Dragon** | Big, round, bright green dragon — looks scary but has sad eyes | Grumpy only because of pain — actually gentle inside | British dragon — has a little Union Jack pennant on its tail |
| **King Reginald** | Fat jolly king, crown slightly wonky | Pompous but kind — loves tea | Always has a teacup in hand |
| **Sir Bigby** | Big beefy knight, very confident | Boastful, runs away at first sight of dragon | Armour is shiny but never been used in battle |

## 🎬 SCENE-BY-SCENE PLAN

### 🎬 SCENE 1 — The Royal Announcement [0:00–0:25]
**Setting:** Castle courtyard — stone walls, flags flying, knights in armour standing at attention.

**Narration (English):**
> *In the great Castle of Kettlemoor, King Reginald had a problem. A rather large, rather noisy, rather fire-breathing problem. A dragon had moved into the hill behind the village, and it roared so loudly every morning that the King's tea went cold before he could drink it. This, said the King, was absolutely unacceptable. He called all his bravest knights to the courtyard.*

**Scene Description:**
- King Reginald stands on the steps, teacup in hand, crown slightly lopsided
- Row of tall knights — and at the very end, tiny Sir Finn whose helmet keeps sliding over his eyes
- King announces: someone must face the dragon
- **COMEDY:** Sir Bigby steps forward proudly — then hears the dragon roar in the distance and steps quietly back into the row

---

### 🎬 SCENE 2 — Sir Finn Volunteers [0:25–0:55]
**Setting:** Castle courtyard — Sir Finn's hand goes up.

**Narration (English):**
> *Nobody moved. The dragon roared again — closer this time. The King's tea splashed right out of his cup. And then — a tiny hand went up at the very end of the row. It was Sir Finn. The smallest, wobbliest, most accidentally brave knight in the whole kingdom. Everyone stared. The King squinted. "Very well," he said, "Sir Finn shall face the dragon."*

**Scene Description:**
- Finn's helmet slides over his eyes as he raises his hand — he pushes it back up
- All other knights look relieved and immediately step back further
- King squints, shrugs, sips his tea — nods
- **COMEDY:** Sir Bigby accidentally lets out a relieved sigh so loud that his visor snaps shut

---

### 🎬 SCENE 3 — The Journey and the Missing Sword [0:55–1:25]
**Setting:** Finn walks the winding road to Dragon's Hill — fields, hedgerows, a red postbox in the middle of nowhere.

**Narration (English):**
> *Sir Finn set off down the road with great determination. His armour clanked. His helmet wobbled. And about halfway up the hill — he reached for his sword. It was not there. He checked his left side. His right side. His back. Nothing. He had left his sword propped against the castle gate. He looked back at the castle — very far away. He looked up at Dragon's Hill — quite close. He took a deep breath and walked on.*

**Scene Description:**
- Finn walking, armour clanking comically
- He pats his side — no sword — double takes
- Checks both sides, behind — nothing
- Looks back at castle (tiny in the distance), looks forward at the hill (close)
- Shrugs, adjusts helmet, walks forward

---

### 🎬 SCENE 4 — Meeting the Dragon [1:25–2:00]
**Setting:** Top of Dragon's Hill — a cave entrance. Smoke curling out. Rumbling sounds.

**Narration (English):**
> *The dragon was enormous. Green and scaly, with smoke puffing from its nostrils and a roar that made the hedges tremble. Sir Finn stood very still. He could run. He probably should run. But then he noticed something that no one else had ever been close enough to see — the dragon was limping. Its great back left foot was red and swollen. And every time it put weight on it, it roared with pain.*

**Scene Description:**
- Dragon emerges from cave — HUGE — Finn looks tiny before it
- Dragon roars — Finn's helmet blows sideways
- Finn stumbles backward, sits down hard
- Then Finn squints and looks at the dragon's foot — red, swollen, a large thorn sticking out
- Finn's expression changes from fear to concern

---

### 🎬 SCENE 5 — The Brave Kind Act [2:00–2:30]
**Setting:** Finn approaches the dragon slowly.

**Narration (English):**
> *Sir Finn did not run. He reached into his armour pocket and pulled out the only thing he had brought with him — his mum's packed lunch. He held out a sandwich as a peace offering, took one careful step at a time, and knelt down beside the dragon's sore foot. Very gently, with both hands, he pulled out the enormous thorn. The dragon froze. And then — it let out a long, low breath — not a roar, but a sigh. The deepest, most relieved sigh anyone had ever heard.*

**Scene Description:**
- Finn holds out half a sandwich — dragon sniffs it suspiciously
- Finn kneels by the foot, grasps the thorn
- Dragon watches with one huge eye
- Finn pulls — the thorn comes out with a POP
- Dragon's expression melts from pain-rage to pure relief
- **COMEDY:** Dragon sneezes with relief — a jet of fire comes out and singes Finn's trousers completely. Finn looks down at his now-smoking shorts, then back at dragon. Dragon covers its mouth with both claws, looking apologetic.

---

### 🎬 SCENE 6 — A New Friendship [2:30–3:00]
**Setting:** Hillside — Finn and dragon sitting together.

**Narration (English):**
> *The dragon lowered its enormous head to the ground beside Sir Finn. And Finn — brave, kind, trouser-less Sir Finn — patted the dragon gently on the nose. The dragon made a sound like a very large cat purring. It pushed the other half of Finn's sandwich back to him with one claw. They sat together on the hill and watched the sunset over Kettlemoor.*

**Scene Description:**
- Dragon curled up, head on grass — Finn sitting beside it, patting its nose
- Dragon is purring — vibrating the whole hillside gently
- They share the sandwich
- Wide shot of the two of them against the golden sunset

---

### 🎬 SCENE 7 — Return to the Castle [3:00–3:30]
**Setting:** Castle courtyard — Finn returns with the dragon walking peacefully behind him.

**Narration (English):**
> *When Sir Finn walked back through the castle gates — with the great dragon walking calmly behind him like a very large, very green dog — every single person in Kettlemoor went completely silent. Then the King dropped his teacup entirely. Sir Bigby fainted on the spot. And the crowd erupted into the biggest cheer the kingdom had ever heard.*

**Scene Description:**
- Gates open — Finn walks in, helmet askew, no trousers left (just armour top and bare legs)
- Dragon wags its tail (Union Jack pennant flapping)
- King drops teacup — it smashes — he doesn't notice
- Sir Bigby faints dramatically into a fountain
- **COMEDY:** Dragon spots a butterfly and tries to follow it, knocking into the castle turret

---

### 🎬 SCENE 8 — The Honour and the Lesson [3:30–3:55]
**Setting:** Throne room — King awards Finn.

**Narration (English):**
> *King Reginald gave Sir Finn the highest honour in the kingdom — the Golden Heart Medal. Not for defeating the dragon with a sword. But for being brave enough to be kind when everyone else was too afraid to look closely. From that day on, the dragon guarded the castle, and Sir Finn was known as the bravest — and kindest — knight who ever lived.*

**Scene Description:**
- King pins medal on Finn's chest
- Dragon sits outside the castle window, peering in happily
- Dragon waves at crowd — crowd cheers — dragon waves too hard and knocks a turret stone loose

---

### 🎬 SCENE 9 — Moral Moment [3:55–4:05]
**Narration (English):**
> *Remember: Being brave doesn't mean having the biggest sword. It means having the biggest heart.*

**Scene Description:**
- Card: **"True bravery is kindness"**
- Finn winks at camera — dragon peers over his shoulder and waves
