# 🐺 M06 — "Tommy Who Cried Wolf in London"
### Kids Moral Story | British Setting | Pixar 3D Animation | Narration Only
### Moral: **Never tell lies — nobody will believe you when it truly matters**
### Duration: ~4.5 Minutes | Age: 3–8 | Comedy Level: ⭐⭐⭐⭐

---

## 📖 STORY SUMMARY

Tommy is a 7-year-old boy in a London neighbourhood who thinks it is hilarious to shout "WOLF!" to get people's attention. He does it three times — and each time, the whole street stops what they are doing: a police constable comes running, Granny Mabel drops her knitting, and shopkeepers close their shutters. Each time it is a lie, and everyone is very cross. Then one afternoon, a real fox (very fox-looking, very sneaky) steals Tommy's packed lunch from the park bench beside him. He shouts "FOX!" and absolutely nobody believes him — and he watches the fox trot cheerfully away with his sandwiches.

## 🎬 SCENE-BY-SCENE PLAN

### 🎬 SCENE 1 — Tommy's Neighbourhood [0:00–0:25]
**Setting:** A classic London street — terraced houses, red buses in the background, a corner shop, Granny Mabel's window, PC Poppleton cycling by, a park at the end.

**Narration (English):**
> *In the busy London street of Bramblegate Row, everyone knew everyone, and everyone was quite content going about their day — until Tommy decided that shouting "WOLF!" was the funniest thing he had ever done in his entire seven years of life.*

**Scene Description:**
- Establishing wide shot of the street — classic London, cheerful
- Tommy walking home from school, looking bored
- He stops, gets a big grin, takes a deep breath

---

### 🎬 SCENE 2 — The First Cry [0:25–1:00]
**Setting:** Street — Tommy shouts.

**Narration (English):**
> *"WOLF!" Tommy shouted, at the top of his lungs, in the middle of Bramblegate Row. What happened next was spectacular. PC Poppleton, who had been cycling peacefully past, swerved so hard he rode straight into a bin. Granny Mabel, sitting at her window, screamed and threw her knitting into the air — it landed perfectly on the postbox. Shopkeeper Singh slammed his shutters shut so fast that he trapped his own scarf in them.*

**Scene Description:**
- Tommy shouts — domino effect
- PC Poppleton swerves, hits bin, helmet falls forward over his face
- Granny Mabel's knitting shoots into the air — lands on postbox
- Shopkeeper Singh pulls shutters — scarf caught — makes muffled sounds
- Everyone gathers around Tommy — where is the wolf?
- Tommy: big grin — "Got you!"
- **COMEDY:** PC Poppleton straightens his helmet with great dignity and gives Tommy the sternest look of his career

---

### 🎬 SCENE 3 — The Second Cry [1:00–1:30]
**Setting:** The next day — everyone slightly warier.

**Narration (English):**
> *The next day, Tommy decided to try again — just to see if it would work twice. It did. PC Poppleton dropped his tea. Granny Mabel threw her knitting again — this time it hit a passing pigeon, who wore it as a hat for several minutes. And Shopkeeper Singh came out with a cricket bat. Everyone was very cross. Tommy laughed and laughed. "You should see your faces," he said.*

**Scene Description:**
- Tommy shouts again — PC Poppleton running out of the police box, tea flying
- Granny's knitting hits pigeon — pigeon wears knitting hat and walks around confused
- Singh with cricket bat, looking around frantically
- Tommy pointing and laughing
- PC Poppleton gives Tommy a very formal warning finger-wag

---

### 🎬 SCENE 4 — The Third Cry [1:30–2:00]
**Setting:** The park.

**Narration (English):**
> *On the third day, Tommy tried it in the park — shouting "WOLF!" from the top of the big oak tree. PC Poppleton arrived panting. Granny Mabel arrived with her knitting already tucked away safely in her bag. Shopkeeper Singh arrived with the cricket bat AND a whistle. When they found Tommy in the tree, grinning, they all simply turned around and went back home. Nobody said a word. The silence, Tommy felt, was worse than being shouted at.*

**Scene Description:**
- Tommy in tree, shouts — everyone arrives
- PC Poppleton: out of breath, red-faced
- Granny Mabel: knitting already in bag, arms folded
- Singh: cricket bat AND whistle around neck — prepared
- They see Tommy grinning — all turn around simultaneously and walk away
- Tommy's grin fades

---

### 🎬 SCENE 5 — The Real Fox [2:00–2:35]
**Setting:** Park bench — Tommy eating lunch.

**Narration (English):**
> *That afternoon, Tommy sat alone on a park bench eating his packed lunch — his favourite: cheese and pickle sandwiches, a bag of crisps, and a biscuit. He had his back to the path, watching some ducks on the pond. He did not notice the fox. The fox — sleek, quiet, carrying a tiny red briefcase — had been watching Tommy's lunchbox from behind a bush. Very smoothly, very professionally, in one lightning-fast move — the fox opened the lunchbox, removed every single item, and disappeared behind the rose bushes.*

**Scene Description:**
- Tommy eating happily, watching ducks
- Fox in background — behind bush, watching — very businesslike
- Fox moves in slow-motion smooth walk — graceful, professional
- Fox opens lunchbox silently, removes items one by one, places them neatly in his briefcase
- Fox tips his head politely at Tommy (Tommy doesn't see), and trots away
- Tommy turns around — empty lunchbox

---

### 🎬 SCENE 6 — Nobody Believes Tommy [2:35–3:10]
**Setting:** Tommy runs up the street shouting.

**Narration (English):**
> *"FOX!" screamed Tommy, running up Bramblegate Row. "A REAL FOX! He stole my lunch! My sandwiches! My crisps! My biscuit!" PC Poppleton did not look up from his newspaper. Granny Mabel kept knitting. Shopkeeper Singh turned away and adjusted a tin of beans on his shelf very carefully. Nobody moved. Nobody believed him. Tommy ran from door to door. Each person gave him exactly the same look: one raised eyebrow.*

**Scene Description:**
- Tommy running, arms waving
- PC Poppleton: newspaper up, one hand waves "yes yes"
- Granny Mabel: keeps knitting without even turning her head
- Singh: puts one tin of beans precisely on a shelf — doesn't acknowledge Tommy
- Tommy's face crumples with complete disbelief
- **COMEDY:** Tommy watches from the end of the street as the fox trots cheerfully past in the background — briefcase swinging — gives Tommy a small formal nod

---

### 🎬 SCENE 7 — The Lesson [3:10–3:40]
**Setting:** Tommy sits on the kerb. PC Poppleton comes and sits beside him.

**Narration (English):**
> *PC Poppleton folded his newspaper and came to sit beside Tommy on the kerb. He said nothing for a moment. Then he told Tommy about the boy who cried wolf — a story that had been around for a very, very long time. When you have spent your trust like pennies — buying laughs at other people's expense — there are none left when you really need them. Tommy looked at his empty lunchbox. Then he looked at PC Poppleton. "I'm sorry," he said. And he meant it.*

**Scene Description:**
- Poppleton sits beside Tommy — friendly, not angry
- Tommy holds empty lunchbox
- Poppleton tells story — warm expression
- Tommy's face shows genuine understanding
- Tommy says sorry — Poppleton nods once, firmly

---

### 🎬 SCENE 8 — Making it Right [3:40–4:05]
**Setting:** Tommy goes to each person and apologises.

**Narration (English):**
> *Tommy went to each person on Bramblegate Row and said sorry — properly, without being told to. Granny Mabel gave him a biscuit from her tin. Shopkeeper Singh gave him a bag of crisps. And PC Poppleton rode back to the station — and came back with a cheese and pickle sandwich. "From the station kitchen," he said. Tommy's trust was back. And from that day on, when Tommy said something — everyone listened.*

**Scene Description:**
- Tommy at each door — apologising
- Granny Mabel: biscuit from tin — pats his head
- Singh: crisps — nods approvingly
- Poppleton returns with sandwich — hands it over
- Tommy smiles — the genuine one, not the grinning one

---

### 🎬 SCENE 9 — Moral Moment [4:05–4:15]
**Narration (English):**
> *Remember: Trust is like a biscuit. Once you've crumbled it, it's very hard to put back together. Tell the truth — every time — and people will always believe you when it matters most.*

**Scene Description:**
- Card: **"The truth is always worth telling"**
- Tommy holds up his lunchbox — it's full again — winks at camera
