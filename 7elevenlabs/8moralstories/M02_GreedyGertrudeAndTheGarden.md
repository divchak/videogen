# 🥧 M02 — "Greedy Gertrude and the Never-Ending Pie"
### Kids Moral Story | British Setting | Pixar 3D Animation | Narration Only
### Moral: **Greed leads to nothing — share what you have**
### Duration: ~4.5 Minutes | Age: 3–8 | Comedy Level: ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐

---

## 📖 STORY SUMMARY

Gertrude is a little girl in a British village who loves pies more than anything — and keeps ALL of them for herself. One day, Gran bakes a magical never-ending pie and leaves it on the windowsill. Gertrude sneaks it all for herself and eats and eats — but the pie never runs out! She eats until her tummy rumbles so loudly that birds fly off the trees, cows run away, and the whole village comes to see what the noise is. In the end, Gertrude learns that sharing a pie tastes far sweeter than eating one alone.

## 🎬 SCENE-BY-SCENE PLAN

### 🎬 SCENE 1 — The Village Cottage [0:00–0:25]
**Setting:** A cosy English village cottage at lunchtime. Rose garden, thatched roof, smoke from chimney. Gran's kitchen window is open — wonderful smells wafting out.

**Narration (English):**
> *In the sunny village of Plumberry, there lived a little girl called Gertrude — and Gertrude loved three things more than anything in the world: pies, pies, and MORE pies. Her Gran made the best pies in all of England, and Gertrude's favourite thing to do was to eat every last crumb before anyone else could get even a SNIFF.*

**Scene Description:**
- Camera zooms into the cottage window — Gran rolling pastry, singing happily
- Gertrude sits at the kitchen table, eyes fixed on the pie on the windowsill, hands under her chin, drooling slightly
- Robin sits on the windowsill next to the pie, watching Gertrude with a suspicious look

---

### 🎬 SCENE 2 — Gran's Magical Pie [0:25–0:55]
**Setting:** Gran's kitchen — warm golden light, jars of jam on shelves, copper pots.

**Narration (English):**
> *But today was different. Today, Gran had made a very special pie. A NEVER-ENDING pie. Gran had used the secret recipe from her great-great-grandmother — a pinch of kindness, a spoonful of sharing, and just a little bit of magic. "This pie," said Gran, "must be shared with the whole village — or something very funny might happen!"*

**Scene Description:**
- Gran places the golden pie on the windowsill — it GLOWS faintly
- Robin looks at the pie, then at Gertrude, then shakes his tiny head slowly
- Gran leaves to visit a neighbour, putting on her hat and coat
- Gertrude inches toward the pie, eyes wide

---

### 🎬 SCENE 3 — Gertrude Takes the Whole Pie [0:55–1:25]
**Setting:** Kitchen — Gertrude alone with the pie.

**Narration (English):**
> *The moment Gran walked out the garden gate, Gertrude grabbed the whole pie. "MINE!" she said. She carried it to her special secret eating chair behind the big armchair. She took one bite... it was DELICIOUS! She took another... and another! But strangely — no matter how much she ate, the pie was always the same size. It was magic indeed!*

**Scene Description:**
- Gertrude runs to her hiding chair, hugging the pie
- She eats a huge slice — the pie magically refills instantly
- Her eyes go wide with glee: she eats faster and faster
- **COMEDY:** Robin peeks around the armchair, shaking his head dramatically — he makes a "tsk tsk" motion with his wing

---

### 🎬 SCENE 4 — The Rumble Begins [1:25–2:00]
**Setting:** Gertrude has been eating for a long time. She is now slumped in the chair, tummy bulging.

**Narration (English):**
> *Gertrude ate and ate and ATE. One hour passed. Then two. The pie never got smaller — but Gertrude's tummy got MUCH bigger. And then — something happened that had never happened before in the history of Plumberry. Gertrude's tummy let out a rumble SO loud, SO enormous, SO thunderous — that three birds fell off the oak tree outside.*

**Scene Description:**
- Gertrude's tummy makes a tiny rumble — she hiccups
- Then a bigger rumble — the jam jars on the shelf rattle
- Then a MASSIVE rumble — the windows shake, a cat falls off a wall outside, three birds shoot off the tree like rockets
- **COMEDY:** Robin, still on the windowsill, holds onto the frame with both wings as the rumble blows him sideways

---

### 🎬 SCENE 5 — The Village Reacts [2:00–2:30]
**Setting:** Village street — various residents reacting to the noise.

**Narration (English):**
> *The rumble rolled through the entire village of Plumberry! Postman Pete's bicycle wobbled so hard his letters flew everywhere. Mrs. Hopkin's prize roses shook and shed all their petals. Two cows ran across the field. And every single child in the village stopped what they were doing and stared at Gran's cottage.*

**Scene Description:**
- Postman Pete cycling along — his letters explode into the air like confetti
- Mrs. Hopkins in the garden — roses explode into petals (she stares, jaw dropped)
- Cows running in a field in the background
- **COMEDY:** A man's hat blows clean off his head and lands on a dog — the dog walks away wearing it proudly

---

### 🎬 SCENE 6 — Gran Returns [2:30–3:00]
**Setting:** Gran walks back through the garden gate to find the whole village at her door.

**Narration (English):**
> *Gran hurried back to find half the village standing at her gate, looking very confused. She pushed through the crowd, opened the door, and found Gertrude — extremely round, extremely green, sitting in the chair with the never-ending pie in her lap. Gran looked at the pie. She looked at Gertrude. She looked at the crowd. And then she began to laugh.*

**Scene Description:**
- Gran opens the door — Gertrude looks up with pie all around her face, looking extremely sorry
- Gertrude's tummy rumbles again — everyone outside takes one step back
- Gran covers her mouth trying not to laugh — fails completely
- Robin does a tiny face-palm

---

### 🎬 SCENE 7 — The Lesson [3:00–3:30]
**Setting:** Gran sits beside Gertrude, puts arm around her.

**Narration (English):**
> *Gran sat beside Gertrude and said nothing for a moment. Then she said: "The magic in this pie only works when it is shared. When you eat it alone, it fills your tummy but never fills your heart. But watch what happens now..." Gran carried the pie to the garden gate and called all the children in.*

**Scene Description:**
- Gran places pie on the garden table
- Children rush in — each gets a slice
- As each child takes a slice, the pie SHRINKS normally — showing it is being used right
- Each child's face lights up — sounds of happy munching

---

### 🎬 SCENE 8 — Gertrude Shares [3:30–4:00]
**Setting:** Garden party — everyone eating pie together.

**Narration (English):**
> *Gertrude watched all the children's happy faces. And then she did something that surprised even herself — she stood up, walked to the table, and cut the very last slice — the best slice, the golden-crusted corner piece — and gave it to the smallest child. And for the first time all day, Gertrude's tummy stopped rumbling. And she felt warm and full — not from pie, but from something much better.*

**Scene Description:**
- Gertrude gives the corner slice to a tiny toddler who beams with delight
- Gertrude's tummy makes a gentle, satisfied sound — like a purr
- Robin does a little happy dance on the fence
- **COMEDY:** Postman Pete appears at the gate with all his letters — "Has anyone seen my letters?" — they all rain down around him from a tree

---

### 🎬 SCENE 9 — Moral Moment [4:00–4:15]
**Narration (English):**
> *Remember: When you share what you have, you don't get less — you get MORE. More friends, more laughter, more happiness. And that is the most magical recipe of all.*

**Scene Description:**
- Animated card: **"Sharing makes everything sweeter"**
- Gertrude waves at camera with jam-covered hands — Robin waves a tiny wing
